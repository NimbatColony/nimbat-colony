Flight Populace


Flight Populace is expressly interested in making sure every Nimbat and Visitant is healthy, happy, and has the things they need to thrive. Taking up the helm on the more political end of things, this Flight prioritizes on ensuring equality for every inhabitant, and to allow them not only to live, but to pursue some level of happiness. In order for this to occur, systems must be put in place to protect rights and opportunities without it coming to cost another’s. Everyone is allowed to live a free life, but regulations are put in place to make sure no one else’s free life can harm another’s. These sects ensure that this remains true.

The Three Sects of Flight Populace

Governance

While laws are primarily made by coordination between Flight Populace and the Colony Council, the Flight itself is not within itself a law-making body. Rather, it is a maintenance body. Policies that protect the rights of Nimbats and Visitants are reviewed every year, and public engagement is utilized to make sure that these policies are functioning properly.

Intervention

There are circumstances in which a complication arises for an individual that may prevent them from having access to something. In these cases, this sect directly intervenes to provide reasonable assistance. Depending on the service, some level of compensation may be required, but it usually does not completely intrude on one’s life and style.

Adaptation

This particular sect works with Flight Progression to overview new developments for the Colony. These developments could have political changes, and so proposals and coordination with the Council are initiated in order to create necessary policies to accommodate for these kinds of changes, without hurting the political core of the Colony.

Notable Members

Jena, Flight Populace Elder

"This Elder displays some of the “weakest” magical prowess in the Colony, but that is not to say it is without use. It just lacks apparentness at first meeting. Simply put, Jena is a telepathic empath. She has the ability to read minds to a simple degree, picking up thoughts that permeate through the mind, as well as emotions. The inhabitants say she can figure out an individual in a little under an hour. Jena uses all of this to cater to inhabitants, especially first-time Visitants. She leaves gifts, offers assuring words or advice, seemingly knowing just what to do or say to try and make one smile, or at least feel comfortable. With abilities and a golden heart like that, I find it easy to see why she represents Flight Populace."

Ela, Governance Sect Leader

“In hindsight, I might have had an easier time pinning down Ela if I had just set up an appointment through her associates. The thing is, she rarely stops moving from facility to facility. Even in the moments I was able to find her, she was quickly occupied by many inhabitants issuing requests, or reports. When I was finally able to find a speaking opportunity with her, I found myself impressed by her ability to have multiple conversations at once. It could be some kind of personal skill, or most likely a kind of enchantment she is practiced in that gives her an easier means of navigating many at once through speech. If your main task revolves around collective decision-making, a skill like that might save a great deal of time.”

Rhia, Intervention Sect Leader

“Rhia being born outside of the Colony had excited my prospects at first, since it opened a possibility of learning of other Nimbat settlements around the world. Unfortunately, she was not too keen on sharing any details beyond a silent affirmation that they do exist. As for Rhia herself, she presents an ability to accurately predict incoming disasters, natural or otherwise. With her proactive knowledge, she begins to procure the resources of the Populace Flight to prepare sensitive areas and individuals for what’s to come. These disasters are not always something as grandiose as tornadoes or floods, it can be something as innocuous as a house fire or complications from heavy winds. Her discomfort with discussing her origins had swayed me away from inquiring on just why she might have left her previous home.”

Jaina, Adaptation Sect Leader

“It only makes sense that it would be the role of the government to determine how foreign technological utilities and methods ought to be incorporated into infrastructure, if they are to be incorporated at all. And the Nimbat that advocates and investigates these kinds of innovations matches my expectations in appearance. Jaina wears a pair of aesthetic lenses, and she’s almost always carrying some kind of primitive electronic device such as a calculator or digital notepad. Our interview took place in her office, which was more akin to a CEO-equivalent in my city. We had many a conversation about the practical applications of objects that wound up on her desk. I worry that she might be easily enticed by cheap “gadgets” that are sold at gas stations.”
