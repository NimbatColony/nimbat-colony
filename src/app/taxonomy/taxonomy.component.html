Nimbat Taxonomy
Nimbats (scientific name Pterofelis Dodrili), are small, winged, felid-like creatures. They can grow to be anywhere between one (1) to four (4) feet tall, from base of the foot to tip of the ear. Their wings are composed of a membranous tissue, similar to bats (genus Chiroptera). They tend to be proportionate to their bodies by other bipedal species' standards. They are born with a total of seven (7) appendages- Two (2) arms, two (2) legs, two (2) wings, and a tail. There are four (4) to five (5) fingers on each hand, and three (3) to four (4) toes on each foot. Paw pads akin to cats exist on the bottoms of their toes, and the tips of their fingers. Their ears are tall and wide. Nimbats tend to live for a very long time; while they develop similar to humans when it comes to maturation cycles, their actual lifespan has an unknowable length.
"I’ve personally met a number of self-proclaimed millennia-old Nimbats, who don’t seem to be much physically older than those who have been alive for no more than 20 years. I’m unsure if I’ll ever know exactly how they work, as Nimbats seem to “dissolve” once they pass away, making dissection practically impossible."
Their eyes can be generalized into three distinct “types”. One of which is pupil and iris-less, and a convex “sparkly” iridescent cornea instead fills the eye. It tends to consist of a gradient of color, from anywhere from blue, to green, to red. Another has a distinctive separate iris and cornea, the iris tending to be a shade of the cornea. The third and final is what would be considered a “typical” eye, with cornea, iris, and pupil all separate. No matter the eye type, no nimbats are defaultly blind. They seem to be completely aware of all visual stimuli, and are able to navigate well.
Additionally, Nimbats who happen to lose parts of their bodies report that their lost parts sparkle and dissolve into the air, and the body that is left behind begins to heal immediately, quickly sealing the wound and stopping blood from escaping. However, they are unable to regrow limbs, and often turn to magic to augment themselves if they find themselves in that situation.
