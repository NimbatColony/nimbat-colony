Flight Preservation


Flight Preservation deals with the Colony’s impact on the world around them, and the world’s impact on the Colony. They work closely with the other Flights to ensure they all are being mindful with their consumption of resources, and the organization of space. They work especially close with Flight Populace, to ensure they’re able to produce enough for Nimbats to prosper, and for Visitants to live without issues. With this coordination established between both Flights, every inhabitant of the Colony should have their needs to live catered to. Should complications arise, problem-solving is delegated to the following sects.

The Three Sects of Flight Preservation

Maintenance

The Maintenance sect focuses on the primary aspect of keeping Colony resources and infrastructural integrity at tip-top upkeep. Before projects can begin, whether it’s Production requiring use of a resource or the construction of a new building, this sect refers to its acute knowledge of the purposes of roads and building codes as well as the reserves of Colony materials in order to reliably come to collected and informed decision-making.

Forestry

This sect is concerned with the use of resources that exist outside of the Colony, and how the Nimbats affect the land around them. Whether it be logging, hunting, or potential Colony expansion, the Forestry sect is the first to inspect and sign off on any activity that may impact the environment outside of the Colony. They are also tasked with forest fire prevention, mostly by clearing the underbrush and felling trees as needed.

Scribing

The record-keepers and historians in this sect have procured vast knowledge of the Colony’s lengthy span. They do not have events from the outside world well-documented unless it has concerned the Colony in the past. The history of the Colony and its related records are created and curated by Scribing with the intent of preserving the knowledge of past eras for future inhabitants, be they Nimbat or Visitant alike. Artifacts and writing from the past are also kept by Scribing, some of which are on public display.

Notable Members

Katell, Flight Preservation Elder

“My interview with the Elder of Preservation did not disappoint. A cataloguer of information and an astute observer of the environs, this adorable Nimbat had much to share with me, happy to elaborate on some of the more natural and historical foundations of the Colony. Getting her to talk about herself, however, was a bit of a hill to traverse. That is when her nervousness took center stage, and on impulse she would cover her muzzle in a document or two. When she is out of her office, she is either hosting her book club or relaxing at home. Public outings are reserved mostly for her close friends and fellow Elders, who are not mutually exclusive. Katell had made it a point to share that she was well-practiced in the magical art of electricity. Perhaps, she just wanted to give off the impression that she had worthy proverbial fangs after her heart-melting timidness was demonstrated to me.”

Artemis, Maintenance Sect Leader

"Artemis is often described as a web-maker. If you ask about something, he knows almost all about it, where to get it, and how they use it. If he doesn’t know about it, it probably just got introduced to the Colony and he is already busily incorporating it into his organized “web” of info. His talents in organization gave him a surefire election into leading this sect. Supposedly when he isn’t working, some say that he secretly enjoys venturing out of the Colony to “see an old friend,” whoever that might be."

Titania, Forestry Sect Leader

“Most Nimbats work closely with their sect leaders. Some have a slightly distant approach, treating the sect leader like an overseer that puts in orders through others, rather than being a manager that stands among their workers. For Titania, she rarely ever sees her fellow peers in Forestry…and that’s for a reason. My patience was already challenged in my attempts to actually locate her, despite many supervisors and informants within Preservation suggesting that I leave her be. Conversing with her was the real hurdle, given that she is inherently socially inept and regards me like I am naught but gentle wind. Titania claims to serve only two beings: the will of the wood, and Katell. The former notion is supported by the fact that she is very much a person well-consumed by nature. Her fur has flecks of dirt and grass, but it still has the serene freshness and smell of spring water and jungle rain. Fireflies often accompany her at night. Her appearance changes with the seasons, making her more like a plant than a Nimbat. Some have even claimed that moss could grow on her. As for speaking, she seems…distant. Her attention is focused mostly on something in the air, which she regards as her priority audience. You are but a lamb to her, and you lack that same privilege. The estranged presentation of talking to a stereotypical seer or an oracle. Even when she makes her monthly reports to Katell, she can be just as off-putting. What in Mother Nature does she do in her free time…?”

Charlotte, Scribing Sect Leader

“Thin, wide-rimmed reading glasses on an older-looking Nimbat that works in Preservation? My first and rightful guess was librarian. What I learned in time is that she is the sect leader and organizer for Scribing. She seems to maintain a more humble outlook to herself, not perusing around her subjects and giving them orders. Instead, she does her work behind her big, clean, cute desk full of knickknacks and turned-in books. Many of the writings found in her library are not of human making—although some are—creating a wondrous opportunity to peek into how Nimbats perceived and recorded the world around them. Not just historical accounts, but stories of expression, or fables for children, or personal ramblings put into story format. Some of them have even been written by Charlotte herself. By her request, she wanted me to include a personal note for any possible reader that might, on the off chance, find my writings:" ”While all the “Checking me out” comments were cute the first few times, it has gotten to be a bit of a bother. Please come up with a more creative and less library-made flirt.”
