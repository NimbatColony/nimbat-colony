Flight Preservation
Flight Preservation deals with the Colony’s impact on the world around them, and the world’s impact on the Colony. They work closely with the other Flights to ensure they all are being mindful with their consumption of resources, and the organization of space. They work especially close with Flight Populace, to ensure they’re able to produce enough for Nimbats to prosper, and for Visitants to live without issues. With this coordination established between both Flights, every inhabitant of the Colony should have their needs to live catered to. Should complications arise, problem-solving is situated to the following sects.

The Three Sects of Flight Preservation

Maintenance

Forestry

Scribing

The Maintenance sect focuses on the primary aspect of keeping Colony resources and infrastructural integrity at tip-top upkeep. Before projects can begin, whether it’s Production requiring use of a resource or the construction of a new building, this sect refers to its acute knowledge of the purposes of roads and building codes as well as the reserves of Colony materials in order to reliably come to collected and informed decision-making.
This sect is concerned with the use of resources that exist outside of the Colony, and how the Nimbats affect the land around them. Whether it be logging, hunting, or potential Colony expansion, the Forestry sect is the first to inspect and sign off on any activity that may impact the environment outside of the Colony. They are also tasked with forest fire prevention, mostly by clearing the underbrush and felling trees as needed.
The record-keepers and historians in this sect have procured vast knowledge of the Colony’s lengthy span. They do not have events from the outside world well-documented unless it has concerned the Colony in the past. The history of the Colony and its related records are created and curated by Scribing with the intent of preserving the knowledge of past eras for future inhabitants, be they Nimbat or Visitant alike. Artifacts and writing from the past are also kept by Scribing, some of which are on public display.

Katell, Flight Preservation Elder
"Katell is by far the most adorable Nimbat Elder described to me. Short like a dumpling, and wearing a pair of big glasses, she’s defined as one of the more “studious” Nimbats in the Colony. Typically buries her nose into a book or a report. Apparently she holds a book club–oh, she’s just a nerdy thing! I hope this report doesn’t fall into her paws, I don’t mean to embarrass her. Even so, her demeanor isn’t by accident. Being an Elder Nimbat for Flight Preservation has put her straight into the more complicated aspects of the very integrity of the Colony, from its structures and systems to its resources. Shockingly, she has control over electricity, which she personally uses to fuel a number of processes, or shock someone that tries to bully her too much."

“Most Nimbats work closely with their sect leaders. Some have a slightly distant approach, treating the sect leader like an overseer that puts in orders through others, rather than being a manager that stands among their workers. For Titania, she rarely ever sees her fellow peers in Forestry…and that’s for a reason. It was a hell of a time trying to locate and interview this Nimbat, and it was even more jarring and weary trying to actually talk with her. Titania claims to serve only two beings: the will of the wood, and Katell. The former notion is supported by the fact that she is very much a person well-consumed by nature. Her fur has flecks of dirt and grass, but it still has the serene freshness and smell of spring water and jungle rain. Fireflies often accompany her at night. Her appearance changes with the seasons, making her more like a plant than a Nimbat. Some have even claimed that moss could grow on her. As for speaking, she seems…distant. Like her attention is focused mostly on something in the air, which she regards as her priority audience. You are but a lamb to her, and don’t have that same privilege. It’s as strange as talking to a stereotypical seer or an oracle. Even when she makes her monthly reports to Katell, she’s just as off-putting. What in Mother Nature does she do in her free time…?”
