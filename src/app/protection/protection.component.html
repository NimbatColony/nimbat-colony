Flight Protection


Flight Protection is tasked with defending and hiding the Colony. They have powerful illusionists, and train some Nimbats to be surprisingly adept fighters, capable of felling beasts much larger than they are. Additionally, when an Advocate returns to the Colony with a new Visitant, Flight Protection is the first to meet with them, to get an understanding as to how they are as a person in order to ensure that they are not dangerous. In some cases, Flight Protection has had some of its members leave the Colony border in order to come to the rescue of an unfortunate passerby under attack.

The Three Sects of Flight Protection

Illusion

As previously mentioned, the illusionist Nimbats are primarily in charge of maintaining the illusory walls that hide the Colony from the public eye. It is impossible to see from the outside. It is impossible to smell, hear, or taste anything from the Colony when one is outside the illusory wall. It’s not even easy to just waltz into the Colony on accident, as several extra illusions are put in place to detract and dissuade travelers and wanderers from stumbling into it. In those rare occasions when someone does arrive, they are deemed “lucky,” and are worthy of investigation. The illusionists also hide other things within the Colony proper.

Defense

In circumstances where violence is witnessed, the Defense sect is usually ready to deal with the threat, utilizing their magical skills in a barrage to subdue or outright eliminate the threat. It is said that when situations are dire, the more pronounced and powerful Nimbats in this sect are brought out to individually handle the situation. The resulting conflict might cause damage to the external parts of the Colony, so priority is given to the rest of the sect to get everyone not engaged in the scuffle out of the area.

Security

The Security sect takes on a similar role to that of Defense, but instead it focuses on domestic matters. Members of the sect wear basic, yet boldly colored half-ponchos as a clearly identifiable uniform. They tend to act as emergency response personnel, handling everything from the rare fire, to search and rescue, to runaway farm animals.

Notable Members

Eislyn, Flight Protection Elder

“Eislyn is perhaps the most public mystery of the Colony. There are articles of theorizing on even the simpler things such as her actual age, her lineage, her diet, and more. The facts we can state with the most certainty, though, is that the Elder of Protection is a master illusionist. When I was given the opportunity to interview her, it was only through a spectral clone that she uses to incur meetings away from where she actually is. She is also rather terse, and prefers to keep to herself, save for official meetings. But it should be said outright that she is not without hobbies— storytelling is among one of her favorite things to do in the “scant moments” of offtime. Supposedly, in her actual office, she has multiple shelves of tales from across the globe.”

Eris, Illusion Sect Leader

“Eris is the more “colorful” illusionist in the Protection Flight. While the Elder that she answers to works with enough subtlety to ease the mind from conspiracy, this Nimbat achieves the same by veering in the polar opposite direction; her illusions are fantastical and her secret-keeping methods are too extravagant to imply that she could be up to something inherently malicious. Even in our interview, she was conjuring an entire change of scenery to give off the impression of a coffee shop, with stained cherry wood tables and the smell of a fresh brew, even though we were moments ago standing on Colony grounds. I do not think she was excited when I politely asked her to bring our atmosphere more down to earth.”

Soteria, Defense Sect Leader

“A great deal of Nimbats do not bother with articles of clothing. Fashion is treated with a bit more utilitarian purpose— that is to say that one’s looks often articulate something or fulfill a task rather than appease a conventional mode of aesthetic and public appearance. Soteria’s lavender cloak is one of the most blunt examples. The desire to look mysterious and powerful is offhanded by the desire to simply keep his body hidden, for his journey of magical empowerment and potentially heretical salves and techniques has left superficial disfigurements that he would personally rather not unveil to “guests.” This Nimbat is indicative of some of the more presentable examples of how terrifying magic can be in the Colony’s most defense-oriented figureheads. For most of our interview, he talked at length about how he wants to inspire growth in his underlings at Defense.”

Marshall, Security Sect Leader

"Marshall is a tall, battle-scarred Nimbat. So tall, in fact, that I honestly mistook her as a Visitant at first. I got to speak at length with her, and she honestly amazed me with her insight on many topics that I felt she would have no idea about. She gave me details on Colony Incident Response Procedures, but asked me not to record anything on paper, for obvious reasons. While her wings are tattered, she’s still able to fly about, albeit slower than smaller and lighter members of her Flight."
