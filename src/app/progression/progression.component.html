Flight Progression


Flight Progression is made up of the most curious and “out-going” Nimbats in the Colony. They reach out to study the outside world, and return to the Colony to report their findings and teachings. The Progression Flight sees that the Colony is able to adapt and progress as they discover new things, be it new teachings over a process or idea, or objects that might prove useful to the Colony. When a Visitant enters the Colony, they're the ones to take their gear and study it to ensure that it is not dangerous, while also looking through potential usage of whatever equipment the Visitant has. For the most part, the sanctity of the Colony’s forest is maintained, and so most technology is rejected if it cannot be recreated in a natural or magical process.

The Three Sects of Flight Preservation

Acquisition

Nimbats within this sect spend most of their lives away from the Colony. They venture out around the world, meeting new people, learning new things, and picking up new objects that fascinate them. At some point they return home, and produce their findings with the rest of the Flight. Not every single thing will be utilized, but those that will wind up going into one of the following sects.

R&D

This sect is concerned with the use of resources that exist outside of the Colony, and how the Nimbats affect the land around them. Whether it be logging, hunting, or potential Colony expansion, the Forestry sect is the first to inspect and sign off on any activity that may impact the environment outside of the Colony. They are also tasked with forest fire prevention, mostly by clearing the underbrush and felling trees as needed.

Education

This sect is designated for teachings brought in by Acquisition. These could range from practical teachings such as new ways to understand math, physics, magic, and so on, to philosophic and political teachings that could contribute to understandings within the Colony. Just like the previous sect, if it is deemed useful and beneficiary without harming the core of the Colony’s values, it may be proposed for incorporation.

Notable Members

Brianna, Flight Progression Elder

“I refuse to step into the main facility for Progression without absolute necessity. Much of its personnel had proved kindly and co-operational, up until I reached the very height of the operation: Brianna. Her office was unkempt and dimly-lit, illuminated only by neon signs on the wall, and her plasma screen television. Snack wrappers and beverage cans were discarded onto the floor. My foot was attacked several times by a Roomba with angry eyes scribbled on it by Sharpie. Brianna herself could not boast of much formality. Almost immediately, she was trying to dig through my pockets and travel bag for anything to satiate her infantile curiosity. My own phone was snatched up by her ability to manipulate metals. Our conversation consisted of surface glance descriptors of her primary tasks—that being commissioning acquisitions from the world beyond the Colony or inviting specific candidates into the Colony for interview. Other than that, she just showed me her gaming consoles and knickknacks around her office space. Before I excused myself in a hasty attempt to leave, I noted that there was a shelf of college-level history textbooks sitting off in the corner. For what purpose is not something I am in the mood to spend mental energy on, at the moment.”

Whisper, Acquisition Sect Leader

“As it turns out, the name “Whisper” is little more than a moniker, and I believe I know why he bothers with such a level of anonymity. To put it bluntly, he seems to fulfill the persona of a black market acquisitioner. That is to say that he can seemingly get anything from anywhere in the world. These are not mere objects of fancy, either. A step into his office revealed a concerning repertoire of items that I do not think a Nimbat of the Colony’s statutes should have. Thermocyclers, 3D printing machines, and canisters of bleach were among these. Much of these items were either pending review, or denied outright. Much of what he was willing to tell me was his tales of daring-do in trying to nab these objects. Thrilling thievery and narrow escapes, either performed by his team or himself. Does he actually enjoy luxury, or just the excitement of the operation?”

Mila, R&D Sect Leader

“A Nimbat in a lab coat was bound to happen, in hindsight. Stepping away from any conversations or discourse about how and why tailors made a lab coat for her size, Mila is easily the most distracted in her line of work. Her associates have recounted many late arrivals to otherwise important meetings on her end. An interview with her, thankfully, was a little more unfocused than I would have hoped. The occasional diatribe of unrelated topics came up, but it was not difficult to bring her back to center. I postulate that her distractedness is a boon as much as it is a curse—her awareness and ability to multitask proves her efficiency in reverse-engineering and configuring the purpose and mechanisms of many objects and devices from the world beyond, at the cost of preoccupying herself from other obligations. She also naps a lot.”

Anne, Education Sect Leader

“Imagine if that favorite elementary school teacher had a federal occupation. That is the most blunt and effective descriptor for Anne that I can put forth. Much of her job orientates around what her sect implies: educating. More specifically, teaching different facilities—Flight-based or independent or otherwise—about any newly-implemented innovations that have been introduced into the Colony. Her magical talent includes a potent telepathy, which she primarily uses to adopt different ways of speaking with individuals from different corners of the globe. Languages, and even accents, are adopted by her dialect in mere seconds. I do not believe that a single sensible person to ever come across the Colony would have legitimate trouble understanding its makeup and goings-on, but the warmth and inclusivity of Anne might provide a pinch of comforting familiarity.”
