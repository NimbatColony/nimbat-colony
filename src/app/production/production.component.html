Flight Production


Flight Production is focused entirely on making sure the colony has the materials they need to continue on. Whether it be food or tools, the Nimbats in Production are the craftsbattes, miners, loggers, and farmers that keep the whole colony moving together.

The Three Sects of Flight Preservation

Agriculture

This sect focuses almost entirely on the production of crops. Whether it be for food or materials like cotton, the farmers and gatherers are the lifeblood of Agriculture for the Colony. Crops are inspected, and those working on the production and maintenance of these crops are compensated accordingly. There are also systems proposed and practiced by this sect in order to protect food security in moments of disaster or destruction.

Metallurgy

A majority of the workers in Metallurgy are focused on extracting and refining material from the Colony’s mine, as well as those more interested in processing metals for tools and other uses around the Colony. What’s most interesting is that while the mine itself isn’t very rich in ore, the Nimbats that deploy their magics for alchemy are able to transmute stone and other procured items from the mines into potential ore. From there, smelters and forgers are able to process that metal into usable material. They work closely with Preservation’s Maintenance sect, providing them with material that may be needed to retain infrastructural integrity for buildings and other projects.

Handicraft

Handicraft is home to the makers, the movers, the shakers, and the improvers that actually produce the things made from raw material found in its sister sects. Carpenters, weavers, goldsmiths, leatherworkers, blacksmiths and more find themselves at-home in this aspect, crafting any manner of goods that may be needed. Some are even open to teaching interested Nimbats and Visitants, and help them improve their handiwork.

Notable Members

“Given the nature of a potential visit with Carolyn, I opted instead to try and gather information on her by proxy to avoid potential unintentional bodily harm.”

Kara, Agriculture Sect Leader

“Kara is the foremost authority on phytomancy and herbology in the Colony. Her power and knowledge with this specific set of skills, magical or otherwise, can often be seen weaved through her day to day existence. Even the very grass she walks tends to grow quicker. She can be found walking about the farmlands, directing and educating workers on nurturing and extracting various crops while she lets her magic weave in and amplify the harvest and its yield. She wears a poncho made of woven string, which was given to her by her Visitant that had passed away years ago. Kara still speaks of them quite lovingly, despite her sometimes-downtrodden mood whenever the topic arises.”

Vivianne, Metallurgy Sect Leader

“Perception can sometimes take different shapes and forms. Some Nimbats here know echolocation, others can see souls and read minds, and others still can feel the weave of nature itself in every corner. Vivianne is of a rare type, picking up a kind of talent that might have been useless in ages past, when the utilization of metal was not very common. Some scholars in the Colony believe that talents like her ability to pick up electromagnetic and ferromagnetic signals from the planet and the objects on it could be a sign of encroaching evolution. Or maybe, magic is just that flexible. The Nimbat working in Metallurgy is a bit of a sassy sort, carrying herself around like she represents or embodies the very gold of the earth, and only reciprocative when other parties seem to believe it. In essence, if you are nice to her by default, she is inclined to be nice to you, usually. Curiously, Vivianne is blind in both eyes, meaning that her ability to sense magnetism and natural magnetic fields is her only real way of seeing. It also gives her an astounding skill in dictating the particles of certain metals, so she could use alchemical processes to transmute.”

Mack, Handicraft Sect Leader

“Perhaps one of the things that makes the crafting sect of the Production Flight a more welcoming experience is that it lays host to a multitude of Nimbats led and guided by a soul who often treats his subordinates with a kindly, fatherly disposition. Mac is a hard-at-work dad, and judging by his looks, one could most certainly make such a guess. Of course, our interview had him frequently talking about his kids, and one work shift with him could turn into a splurge of photos and stories. I find it hard not to smile at how actually compassionate he is. The only sore item of note is his severely-damaged wings. They stay tucked in most of the time, for the entire membranous fiber has been burned and torn in places, making flight impossible for him. In spite of this, he proves to be one of the heartiest Nimbats in the Colony.”
